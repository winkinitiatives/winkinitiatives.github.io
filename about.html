Our story

Worship (through) Industry, Nurturing Knowledge

Wink Initiatives exists to honour God through the work of technology while creating tangible opportunity for people, partners, and communities. Though we are not a faith-based organisation, our founder established this collective as a response of gratitude to Jesus Christ for every door that was opened. We believe industry is an act of worship when it nurtures knowledge, opens doors for others, and delivers excellence that improves everyday life.

Technology is the primary engine that powers every other industry we touch. From software delivery to mentorship, we channel technical skill into practical impact, always pointing the credit back to Jesus Christ while welcoming collaborators from every background.

The network we are building is relational at its core—partners, talent, and alumni who support each other, compound their knowledge, and design solutions that uplift their clients and communities.

Industry as worship

We pursue excellence in delivery because great products, disciplined stewardship, and trustworthy partnerships are expressions of gratitude for the opportunities we have been given.

Opportunities for people

Training, apprenticeships, and real product work create pathways for emerging technologists to build careers while serving real customer needs.

Networked impact

We cultivate a family of partners and friends committed to supporting one another and expanding positive influence across Africa and beyond.

Tech Solutions

We plan, design, and ship resilient digital products that address real organisational needs while keeping users and stakeholders at the centre.

  • Product discovery and roadmaps grounded in measurable outcomes
  • Cross-functional engineering squads delivering secure, scalable software
  • Lifecycle support that keeps platforms healthy long after launch

Tech Training & Mentorship

We nurture knowledge by pairing classroom learning with real delivery, equipping talent to create impact within their own teams and communities.

  • Mentorship programs that blend discipleship-minded values with practical skills
  • Hands-on projects, code reviews, and peer learning cohorts
  • Opportunities that connect graduates to trusted partners and real client work
